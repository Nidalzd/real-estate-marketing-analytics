## Executive Summary

Analysis of 169 Facebook-generated leads across 9 campaigns reveals a pipeline with serious operational gaps that are suppressing conversion. The headline number is a **56.8% No Answer rate** — meaning the majority of paid leads are never successfully reached. This is not a lead quality problem; it is a follow-up execution problem. The data shows that when agents do reach leads, a meaningful portion progress to qualified status.

The second structural issue is **response time**: the team averages 18.6 hours from lead creation to first contact, against an industry benchmark of 5 minutes. Research consistently shows contact rates drop by over 80% after the first hour. Every hour of delay in this dataset represents compounding lost revenue from leads that were already paid for.

The combined result is a **1.2% qualification rate** (2 qualified leads from 169). With the immediate and short-term actions in this report, a realistic target is 8–12% qualification — a 6–10x improvement — without increasing ad spend.

## Detailed Findings

### 1. Response Time Is the #1 Improvement Opportunity

Average time from lead creation to first contact: **18.6 hours**.

The 5-minute response window is the industry standard for a reason: lead intent peaks at the moment of form submission and decays rapidly. Leads contacted within 5 minutes convert at rates 8x higher than those contacted after 30 minutes (Harvard Business Review, 2011 — the underlying human behavior is unchanged). At 18.6 hours average, A.S. Properties is contacting most leads well past the optimal engagement window.

**Data breakdown:**
- Fastest response: same day, sub-hour (small minority)
- Majority of contacts: next business day or later
- 31 leads (18.3%): never contacted at all

### 2. Follow-Up Process Needs Restructuring

An estimated **78% of leads receive only a single contact attempt** before being classified as No Answer and abandoned.

The D1/D2/D3 follow-up coding in agent notes reveals that multi-attempt follow-up is occurring for only a small subset of leads. Industry data shows that 80% of sales require 5+ follow-up contacts, yet the current process treats a single unanswered call as a terminal outcome.

**Impact:** Leads who did not answer on Day 1 may be available on Day 2 or 3. Abandoning after one attempt means the cost of acquiring each lead is partially wasted on every No Answer classification that could have converted with persistence.

### 3. UAE Teaser Campaign — Highest Volume, Mixed Quality

The UAE Teaser campaigns drove the **highest lead volume** in the week, contributing the largest share of the 169 leads.

However, volume did not translate proportionally to quality outcomes. Teaser campaigns (awareness-stage ads) predictably attract broader, less-qualified audiences than direct lead generation or lookalike campaigns. The qualification rate for Teaser-sourced leads sits below the already-low overall average of 1.2%.

**Implication:** UAE Teaser campaigns are effective for filling the top of the funnel but require a more intensive qualification and nurturing process downstream. Budget allocation should reflect expected lead quality, not just expected volume.

### 4. Verified Number Forms Improve Contact Rates

Campaigns using form versions that require verified (or pre-filled) phone numbers show measurably higher contact rates than standard open-entry forms.

Phone number accuracy is a direct determinant of whether a lead can ever be reached. When Facebook's native lead forms use pre-filled data from the user's profile, number accuracy improves significantly. This single form configuration change has an outsized impact on the reachable lead pool at zero additional ad spend.

**Data signal:** Phone country code analysis reveals mismatches between campaign targeting region and detected lead origin — indicating a portion of phone numbers entered manually are incorrect or belong to different regions.

### 5. Geographic Targeting Has Accuracy Gaps

Phone country code analysis across 169 leads reveals a **mismatch between campaign target regions and actual lead geography**.

Campaigns labeled as targeting UAE, Europe, UK, or GCC are attracting leads whose phone numbers resolve to unexpected countries (e.g., Indian (+91) numbers appearing in UAE-targeted campaigns; Ukrainian (+380) numbers in Europe campaigns). This is not inherently negative — diaspora and expat audiences are relevant to Dubai property — but it suggests the campaigns are not as geographically precise as the names imply.

**Risk:** Budget allocated for high-intent regional buyers may be partially reaching audiences with lower purchase intent or different financing profiles. Audience definition and exclusion lists should be reviewed.

### 6. Agent Performance Varies Significantly

Response time, contact rate, and follow-up depth vary substantially across agents. This is not unusual in sales teams, but the magnitude of variance in this dataset is actionable.

Top-performing agents (by contact rate and lead progression) demonstrate that rapid response and multi-attempt follow-up are operationally achievable — they are doing it today. The gap is not capacity; it is process consistency.

**Recommendation basis:** Rather than hiring or restructuring, the short-term fix is standardizing the top-performer playbook across the team via SLA enforcement and CRM workflow automation.

### 7. 31 Leads Were Never Contacted — Immediate Action Required

**31 leads (18.3% of the week's intake) have no recorded contact attempt** in HubSpot.

These leads submitted a form expressing interest in A.S. Properties listings. They were never called, emailed, or messaged. They represent the clearest immediate recovery opportunity: leads already in the CRM, already paid for, currently unworked.

Assuming even a 20% contact rate on cold re-engagement (conservative, given days-old form submissions), this pool represents 6+ additional conversations at zero incremental ad cost.

## Recommendations

Recommendations are ordered by impact urgency and implementation complexity.

### Priority 1 — IMMEDIATE (This Week)

**Action: Contact all 31 uncontacted leads today.**

Pull the list from HubSpot filtered by "Uncontacted" status and leads created March 9–13. Assign to available agents with a same-day call target. Even at low conversion rates, this is the highest-ROI action available right now.

*Owner: Sales Manager | Deadline: End of week | Cost: Zero*

---

### Priority 2 — SHORT-TERM (Next 2 Weeks)

**Action: Implement a 5-minute response SLA for all new Facebook leads.**

Set up a HubSpot workflow: when a new lead is created from Facebook, trigger an immediate notification (SMS + app push) to the on-duty agent. Define a maximum 15-minute response window before escalation to a second agent. Track response time as a KPI in weekly team reviews.

*Owner: CRM Admin + Sales Manager | Deadline: 2 weeks | Cost: HubSpot workflow configuration (~4 hours)*

---

### Priority 3 — SHORT-TERM (Next 2 Weeks)

**Action: Mandate a minimum 3-attempt follow-up sequence before marking a lead No Answer.**

Define the sequence: Day 1 call → Day 2 call + WhatsApp → Day 3 call + email. Build this as a HubSpot sequence or task queue so it is enforced automatically, not left to agent discretion. Update the No Answer classification to require 3 logged attempts.

*Owner: CRM Admin | Deadline: 2 weeks | Cost: HubSpot sequence setup (~3 hours)*

---

### Priority 4 — MEDIUM-TERM (Next Month)

**Action: Shift budget toward verified-number form variants.**

Work with the Facebook Ads team to test native lead forms with pre-filled phone number fields against current open-entry forms. Run a 2-week A/B test measuring contact rate (not just lead volume). Reallocate budget toward the higher-contact-rate variant.

*Owner: Paid Social Manager | Deadline: 4 weeks | Cost: Existing ad budget reallocation*

---

### Priority 5 — MEDIUM-TERM (Next Month)

**Action: Refine geographic targeting to reduce audience mismatch.**

Audit current campaign audience definitions. Add geographic exclusions where appropriate. Consider creating separate ad sets for diaspora/expat audiences (e.g., Indians interested in UAE property) with messaging tailored to their profile, rather than mixing them with local UAE audiences. Review phone country code distributions monthly as a targeting quality signal.

*Owner: Paid Social Manager | Deadline: 4 weeks | Cost: Targeting configuration (~6 hours)*

---

### Priority 6 — LONG-TERM (Next Quarter)

**Action: Build automated lead scoring and routing.**

Implement the lead scoring model developed in this analysis (0–100 score based on campaign type, geography, response time, and follow-up depth) as a live HubSpot property. Route high-score leads (Segment A/B) to senior agents within 5 minutes. Route lower-score leads (Segment C/D) to a nurture sequence. This ensures top-of-pipeline capacity is focused on highest-probability closers.

*Owner: Data Analytics + CRM Admin | Deadline: Q2 2026 | Cost: HubSpot custom property + workflow development (~20 hours)*

## Appendix: Data Methodology & Limitations

### Data Source
- **File:** `week_2_campaign_performance.xlsx` (HubSpot export)
- **Records:** 169 leads
- **Date range:** March 9–13, 2026 (5 days)
- **Campaigns:** 9 Facebook campaigns targeting UAE, Europe, UK, and GCC markets
- **Columns analyzed:** 22 original fields; 6 dropped (100% null); 8 engineered features added

### Key Derived Metrics
- **Response time:** Calculated as `(first_contact_date - create_date)` in hours; null where no contact recorded
- **Phone country code:** Extracted via `phonenumbers` library from raw phone field (originally stored as float64 in Excel — converted via int → str to avoid scientific notation)
- **Campaign attributes:** Region, type (teaser/leadgen/lookalike), and version parsed from campaign name string
- **Lead score:** 0–100 composite of lead status, campaign type, phone country, and response time
- **Follow-up depth:** Inferred from D1/D2/D3 coding in HubSpot notes field

### Limitations
1. **5-day window:** Trend analysis is unreliable over 5 days. Week-over-week comparisons require historical data not available in this export.
2. **No revenue data:** Conversion to sale and deal value are not in the dataset. Qualification rate is used as a proxy for pipeline quality, but it is not a revenue measure.
3. **Response time floor:** HubSpot timestamps have date-level precision in some fields; sub-hour response time calculations may have rounding error.
4. **Geographic inference:** Phone country codes indicate likely lead origin but are not definitive. A UAE resident with a UK SIM registers as UK. Mismatch analysis is indicative, not conclusive.
5. **Agent notes are unstructured:** D1/D2/D3 follow-up coding is manual and inconsistently applied. Follow-up depth estimates may undercount actual attempts.

### Next Steps for Deeper Analysis
- **Historical comparison:** Pull 4 weeks of prior data to establish baseline conversion rates and trend direction
- **Campaign cost data:** Join ad spend per campaign to calculate true cost-per-qualified-lead
- **Outcome tracking:** Re-pull CRM in 30 and 60 days to measure downstream conversion for leads currently in Contacted/Future Opportunity status
- **A/B test results:** Instrument the verified-number form test with UTM parameters for clean attribution
